# Final review checklist

## Cross-document consistency

- Does the specification describe the same software as the source attachment?
- Does the registration-form draft reuse the same name, version, environment, and function summary?
- Do file counts and line counts agree everywhere they appear?

## Specification checks

- Is the software boundary clear?
- Are chapter titles still necessary, or can some be merged or removed?
- Is every diagram readable and non-overlapping?
- Does the tone remain normal specification prose throughout?
- Are all major claims backed by code?

## Source attachment checks

- Are the selected files truly the core chain?
- Is the ordering aligned with the explanation order?
- Is the line count rechecked after the final selection?
- Is the attachment formatting readable in Word?

## Registration-form checks

- Are uncertain legal or identity fields left blank?
- Are hardware, OS, language, and development environment statements realistic?
- Does the "software major functions" text match the specification summary?

## Cleanup

- Remove TODOs, drafting notes, and contradictory alternatives.
- Archive excluded drafts instead of leaving them mixed with final material.
- Keep final file names stable and submission-oriented.
